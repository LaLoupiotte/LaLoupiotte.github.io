Title: Don't think, just do
Subtitle: This simple sentence can enhance the way you are approaching a new project or habit.
Filename: just_do
Img: justdo.jpg
quote: Don't think, just do
quote: Just do it!
You might have heard these sentences without reflecting on them, without noticing how they can change the way you act when overthinking.
A Photographer called Liam uses this phrase to entice his community to go out and take photos without thinking about it.
Just start! You'll figure out later how to respond to encountered problems.
If you're learning photography like me, don't start by searching what the best camera or lens is the best because none of them is perfect anyway. Take your phone or buy an average camera with a lens suited for almost everything (50mm) and take photos. Bring your camera everywhere take pictures of everything. Try thinking outside of the box. The time spent searching for a good lens isn't as valuable as one hour of street photography.
Doing this may seem obvious, in reality, a lot of people don't. It's easier to watch videos about the thing you want to do rather than doing it. Our brain tends to do more comfortable things. The thing is that your first photo or youtube video won't be good. I'm a beginner writer, so as a result, my articles suck. The thing is that I don't mind it. I know that if I keep writing, my work will be better. Writing a couple of sentences every day taught me more than watching or reading about other people's content. When you start running, you'll always finish closer to the finish line than before. Even if it's only by 100 meters, you have made progress! And this is what matters. You didn't do it perfectly, nobody does, but you did it. Keep in mind that a lot of people don't even do the 100 meters.
The next time you are about to start a new project, try switching from a thinking mindset to a doing mindset.
Remember that the more you do, the better you'll get at doing, and this is true in every domain.
