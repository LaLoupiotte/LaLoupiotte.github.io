I have read 14 books in two months. What have I learned ?

This summer, I gave myself a challenger: What if I could read a book a week for two months. It turns out that I did it and read some bonus novels.

Posted by Guilherme Miranda Martins on Sep 13, 2021

Growing up, I wasn't an avid reader. I preferred playing with my friends. However, this trend changed as I grew up. Not that I don't spend time with my friends but because I read a lot more now. To do so, I had to find books that piqued my interest.

Here are the books that I read during these two months.

Reading (fiction) is not productive.

When sitting down to read, I found myself feeling productive for no reason. It may be better than scrolling on Tiktok, but it isn't studying either. When I was too lazy to do work, I just picked up a random book and felt like I was progressing even though I was not. It was hard for me to comprehend that reading fiction was procrastination. Read during your free time.

Bestsellers aren't usually compelling.

To be clear, I'm not saying that they are bad books. The thing is that I often found myself checking and buying ones only because they were bestsellers everywhere, and in the end, they were boring for me. I didn't like them. The good stuff I read was just on a shelf in a random library.

When reading something that we just found by ourselves, we don't have crazy expectations like others that have won prizes. In my case, the best books I've read are not bestsellers.

Books make you travel.

Different worlds, persons, and relationships:

I didn't expect to be taken to different worlds so smoothly. When going through a book, our brain imagines what is going on. We are living the moment. We are the main characters.

Finishing a book is like coming back from vacation. You feel sad.

Benefits.

I experienced that reading before bed made me fall asleep earlier. The screen on our phone doesn't let our intern clock function properly because of the blue light. This little benefit can improve your life. The quality of your sleep defines the quality of your day.

Non-fiction.

Non-fiction books are great. They sum up in a couple of pages years of work for the author.

Atomic Habits explains what leads to creating a new habit and how to do so. The author's knowledge about the subject is in the book. When actively reading it, you learn a LOT about how your behavior could change.

Don't finish what you don't like.

Sometimes, you don't like what you are reading. The plot, the universe doesn't seem right.

It happened a couple of times for me now. But still, I wanted to finish. Most of the time, when ending a chapter, I felt frustrated. At least it didn't ruin my habit of reading. In my case, not finishing the titles I didn't like was game-changing. Reading became more pleasant. I didn't feel any pressure when buying books.
