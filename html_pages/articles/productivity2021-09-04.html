Simple ways to be more focused

In a world where distractions are everywhere, these tips can highly benefit your ability to focus.

Posted by Guilherme Miranda Martins on Sep 04, 2021

Adjust your study space.

If you are at home, tidying up your workspace every time you leave will reduce your procrastination as well as improve your focus. When something is clean, you won't be distracted by it.

Adjust the temperature of your room to 22°C (72°F) when possible.

Being organized directly impacts our performance. Instead of focusing on what we have to do, we focus on doing.

You might have noticed a similar path in the anterior tips. You have to avoid frictions. If your brain is distracted, it is hard to be efficient. Inefficiency leads to more time studying, and our ability to focus decreases the more time we spend on a task.

Phones: distraction at its best.

Our use of social media is very high. We have built the habit of checking them consistently. When nearby, our brain tends to want this quick dopamine hit by scrolling through our feed. This quick-minute distraction affects our focus.

To prevent this, you have multiple options. The three that worked for me are logging out of your accounts and not saving the password. It can create enough friction for you to stop checking them every two minutes. Uninstall the apps on your phone. Use an app like Forest. It's simple to use. The user plants an X-minute tree, and if he goes to another application during this time, the tree dies. It's psychological. You don't want to see a dead tree in your forest.

Plan, set to-dos.

When you think about doing homework or just something random, build the habit of putting it on a to-do list. Set little things to do like decluttering your laptop or doing math homework. However, don't write your long-term goals there. Furthermore, this method is more effective if you can see your to-dos. If you create this simple habit, you won't need to think about doing it. You'll take a look at your to-do list and do the stuff you need.

If we don't know what to do, we tend to procrastinate and do nothing. That's why to-do lists are effective.

Give meditation a go.

Meditation has a lot of benefits. One of them is improved focus. Try free trials of headspace or 7mind to understand the basics. You might not see direct results after one week of meditation. Using Forest combined with daily meditation has skyrocketed my focus. I can guarantee that you will see the benefits too.

Music

You have to be careful with this tip.

Listening to a type of music while being focused will create a path in your brain. This music equals focus. However, if you use the same songs for partying and studying, your ability to focus will be damaged when listening to them. Try different things, lofi music is popular. I listen to Fado ( Portuguese music genre).

Remember that you should listen to it only when studying.

Simple points

You should aim at sleeping between 7 to 8 hours of sleep every day.

Have eaten something not to be hungry during study time.

Have a bottle of water nearby to stay hydrated.

If possible, disconnect from the Internet.

If you want exercises to improve your concentration, know that they can be fun.

Board games are proven to be good at improving your ability to focus.

Exercising doesn't only train your body but also your brain. There is a link between exercising and improved focus.

Be careful.

Do not try to implement every tip directly into your life. You will be much more likely to drop those habits in a short period.

I recommend applying these tips but only one at a time, start with Pomodoro, then after one or two weeks, install Forest, etc.
