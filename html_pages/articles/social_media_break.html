Being away from social media for 30 days was not life-changing.

Posted by Guilherme Miranda Martins on August 25, 2021

As a tech lover, I love doing projects and creating programs on my computer. However, my creativity was at its lowest, procrastination began. As a result, I did not code for an entire month. Additionally, my screen time hit 4 hours per day. I had to take a break from social media. It was not the first time I deleted Instagram, Twitter, and Snapchat. It was refreshing. Learning new programming languages and doing experiences on laptops was how I decided to use my gained time.

Unexpectedly, the old patterns came again only a few weeks after. The positive effects of the experience were gone. I thought that by doing this experience once more, my creativity would skyrocket like the first time, but it was not the case. I found myself procrastinating less though my creativity stagnated at a low level.

As I'm writing this, two months passed since I did this experience. My creativity is at its highest point ever for a simple reason. I'm spending time in my grandparents' home where the Internet is not part of their lives. Since I don't have access to social media, it's a forced break from the connected world. My weeks were composed of workouts and reading time. Now that I'm back on the web, I see projects everywhere. My notebook is plain of new ideas and projects.

Depending on how much you use your phone, being away from your phone can or cannot be the best solution to increase your creativity. Why don't you give it a shot anyway? Other benefits can appear from this break from the Internet, such as an increased inner peace or a significant gain in productivity.
